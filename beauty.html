Hairspray

  • 1/2 Cup Water
  • 2 Tsp Sugar
  • 2 Tbsp Rubbing Alcohol or Vodka
  • 4-6 Drops Essential Oils (opt)
  1. Heat water to a boil and turn off heat source.
  2. Add 2 tsps of sugar and stir until completely dissolved.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons rubbing alcohol.
  4. Add 4-6 drops of your favorite essential oil scent (optional).

*Tips*

  • This batch will fill up two travel spray containers which can be found at Walmart.
  • After applying hairspray, wait until hairspray is dry before combing or applying additional hairspray.
  • The hairspray hold can be adjusted by increasing or decreasing the amount of sugar.
  • If you're concerned about ants, which I haven't had any issues, add your favorite essential oil to repel them.

Hairgel

  • 1/4 Tsp Unflavored Gelatin
  • 1/2 Cup Hot Water
  • 4-6 Drops Essential Oils (opt)
  • Aloe Vera for moisture (opt)
  1. Heat water and mix together with gelatin in a small bowl.
  2. Stir well to combine and refrigerate for about 1 hour, or until set.
  3. Once cooled and set, add essential oils if desired, and stir to combine.
  4. Using a funnel, transfer to a small squeeze bottle for easiest dispensing or mason jar.

*Tips*

  • This batch will fill up one travel lotion container which can be found at Walmart.
  • Gel hold can be adjusted by adding more or less gelatin.
  • Test first using a small amount of hairgel until you achieve your desired look.

Chapstick

  • 2 Tbsp Beeswax
  • 1 Tsp Coconut Oil
  • 2 Tbsp Shea Butter
  • 1/4 Tsp Vitamin E
  • Vanilla Bean (opt)
  1. Melt beeswax in a small pan over low heat. If using vanilla bean, cut bean long-ways to expose contents and place entire bean in small pan.
  2. Add coconut oil and shea butter until all ingredients a melted.
  3. Using a small medicinal dropper or a turkey baster, pour the melted liquid into your lip balm containers.
  4. Cool completely to harden.
  5. Cover with cap and decorate or label as desired.

*Tips*

  • This is very moisturizing chapstick. For harder consistency, use less coconut oil and increase beeswax.
  • Chapstick containers are inexpensive and can be found on Amazon or your local hobby store.

Lotion

  • 1/2 Cup Almond oil or Jojoba oil
  • 1/3 Cup Coconut Oil
  • 1/4 Cup Beeswax
  • 1 Tsp Vitamin E
  • 1 Tsp Aloe Vera for moisture (opt)
  • 2 Tbsp Shea Butter or Cocoa Butter (opt)
  • 4-6 Drops Essential Oils (opt)
  1. Combine almond (or jojoba oil), coconut oil and beeswax in a small pot on the stove. If using shea or cocoa butter, add it as well.
  2. Heat on low until the ingredients will start to melt. Stir occasionally as they melt.
  3. When all ingredients are completely melted, add vitamin E oil (if using) and any essential oils.
  4. Using a funnel, transfer to a small squeeze bottle for easiest dispensing or mason jar.
  5. Use within 6 months for best moisturizing benefits.

*Tips*

  • This batch will fill up one travel lotion container which can be found at Walmart.
  • This lotion is very moisturizing. A little goes a long way.
