Laundry Soap

  • 1 Cup Borax
  • 1 cup Arm & Hammer Washing Soda (not baking soda)
  • 1 Bar Dr. Bronners Bar Soap (scent of your choosing)
  1. Using a cheese grater or food processor, convert bar soap into shavings.
  2. Mix with Borax and washing soda.
  3. Pour into resealable storage container.
  4. To use, 1 coffee scoop (1/8 cup) for small loads. 1.5 to 2 coffee scoops for large loads.

*Tips*

  • This soap is safe and usable for HE washing machines.
  • A food processor can be used instead of grating soap bar by hand.
  • If grating soap by hand, small pieces can be made smaller by shaking the storage container after everything is mixed.

Furniture Polish

  • 1 Cup Olive Oil
  • 1/4 Cup White Vinegar
  • 4-6 Drops Lemon or Orange Essential Oils (opt)
  1. Add all ingredients to a spray bottle and shake well.
  2. Spray onto a lint-free cloth and wipe onto furniture for light polishing.

*Tips*

  • Empty spray bottles are inexpensive and can be purchased from Lowe's or Walmart and are typically found in the cleaner aisle.
  • For a heavier gloss, spray directly onto furniture and buff to a shine.
  • Shake between uses.

Glass Cleaner

  • 1/4 Cup Rubbing Alcohol or Vodka
  • 1/4 Cup White Vinegar
  • 1 Tbsp Cornstarch
  • 2 Cup Warm Water
  1. Combine everything in a spray bottle, and shake well.
  2. Spray on glass and wipe clean with cloth or paper towel.

*Tips*

  • Empty spray bottles are inexpensive and can be purchased from Lowe's or Walmart and are typically found in the cleaner aisle.
  • The cornstarch is what gives this cleaner extra fighting power.
  • Shake between uses.

Poison Ivy Kill

  • 3 Cups Vinegar
  • 1/2 Cup Salt
  • 1 Tbsp Liquid Dishsoap
  1. Dissolve salt in vinegar and then add dishsoap.
  2. Pour into spray bottle and spray onto poison ivy leaves.
  3. Repeat after one week if necessary.

*Tips*

  • Empty spray bottles are inexpensive and can be purchased from Lowe's or Walmart and are typically found in the cleaner aisle.
  • Shake between uses.
  • Best to not apply before rain is expected.
  • I noticed this also kills ants dead in their tracks.

Ant Bait

  • 3 Cups Sugar
  • 1 Cup Water
  • 2 Tbsp Borax
  1. Mix all in a saucepan and bring to a boil for 3 minutes, stirring constantly.
  2. Once cool (or not piping hot), bait will thicken.
  3. Pour into black squeeze bottles.
  4. Place a drop on a plastic disc or cardstock as traps.

*Tips*

  • Plastic squeeze bottles are inexpensive and can be found in the hobby section of Walmart or in most hobby stores.
  • Do not kill any ant lines that are coming or leaving the trap. The ants will take the bait back to their nest where the Borax will kill the entire colony.
  • If placing traps outside, make sure the traps are out of the rain.
